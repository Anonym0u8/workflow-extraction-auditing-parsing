# workflow-extraction-auditing-parsing
100% of code written by Claude. This very well could be just stupid and useless. Evolved and devolved multiple times through prompting and scope decisions. Intentions of adding GUI, pruning, deduplication, recommendations, ecosystem tagging. 

Creation and testing was done on a sample size of a little under 500 of the highest rated workflows from the past month and year downloaded from CivitAI. 

The original intention was tracking node frequency and usage as well as their connections across workflows in order to better understand 'good' practices involved in workflow creation. With the goal being large personal AIO workflows, as I have a preference for them over hyper-specific workflows that accomplish a single task.

I suppose that was accomplished - at least to a degree - but I'm wondering about the possibility of expanding the usage to handling large workflow collections and reducing the bloat that occurs over time when downloading workflows for some people. I have no problem admitting that even getting to this point is well beyond my personal capability and creating something functional, complete, and useful from here will be even further outside of that. It will likely also require LLM integration since fingerprinting and deterministic structure will only go so far. 

I'm unsure if, how or when I will approach any of this. But if one person finds this useful as-is, then it wasn't a complete waste of time.
